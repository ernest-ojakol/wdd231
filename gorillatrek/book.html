1 Person Trek

  • Exclusive Experience: Enjoy a private, one-on-one encounter with the gorillas, allowing for a deeply personal and uninterrupted interaction.
  • Customized Itinerary: Tailor your trek to your pace and interests, with the flexibility to spend more time observing specific behaviors or taking photographs.
  • Enhanced Safety: With just one trekker, your guide can focus entirely on your safety, ensuring a more secure and attentive trekking experience.
  • Premium Guide Attention: Receive undivided attention from your expert guide, who can provide in-depth knowledge on gorilla behavior, conservation, and local flora and fauna.
  • Lower Environmental Impact: A single person trek minimizes the impact on the gorilla habitat, promoting conservation by reducing human presence.
  • Intimate Photography Opportunities: Capture unique, unobstructed photos and videos without other trekkers in the frame, enhancing your photography portfolio or personal memories.
  • Quiet Observation: The absence of group noise allows for a quieter trek, potentially leading to more natural gorilla behaviors and closer encounters.
  • Direct Interaction: With no competition for attention, you can have direct, meaningful interactions with your guide, asking questions and learning more about gorilla conservation efforts.
  • Flexibility in Timing: Easier to schedule at your convenience, possibly allowing for early morning or late afternoon treks when animal activity might be higher.
  • Personalized Conservation Contribution: Your solo trek fee might contribute more directly to conservation efforts, as the costs are not spread among a larger group.

2 People Trek

  • Shared Exclusive Experience: Enjoy a more intimate experience with just one other person, allowing for a quieter and more personal interaction with the gorillas.
  • Customized Experience for Two: Tailor the trek to suit both participants' interests and pace, providing flexibility in how you spend your time with the gorillas.
  • Enhanced Safety and Companionship: Trekking with a partner provides companionship, making the experience more enjoyable, while still maintaining a high level of safety due to the small group size.
  • Quality Guide Interaction: With only two trekkers, your guide can offer personalized attention, sharing detailed insights into gorilla behavior, conservation efforts, and the surrounding ecosystem.
  • Reduced Environmental Impact: A trek with just two people still minimizes the environmental footprint, contributing positively to conservation efforts.
  • Photography Opportunities: Capture photos and videos with minimal interference, with your companion possibly helping with shots or posing together in the wild setting.
  • Quiet Observation: With fewer people, the trek remains quiet, which can lead to more natural gorilla behavior and potentially closer encounters.
  • Shared Learning Experience: Both participants can engage in direct, meaningful interactions with the guide, enhancing the educational aspect by discussing and learning together about conservation.
  • Flexibility in Timing: Scheduling is easier with just two, allowing for optimal timing based on your mutual availability, possibly aligning with peak gorilla activity times.
  • Conservation Contribution: Your shared trek fee still contributes significantly to gorilla conservation, with the advantage of sharing the cost while maintaining the exclusivity of the experience.

3 People Trek

  • Small Group Intimacy: With just three participants, the experience remains intimate, allowing for a close-knit group dynamic while still offering a personal encounter with the gorillas.
  • Customized Group Experience: The trek can be tailored to the interests and physical abilities of the three participants, ensuring everyone enjoys the experience at their preferred pace.
  • Enhanced Safety with Companionship: A group of three provides safety in numbers with the added benefit of companionship, making the trek more enjoyable and less intimidating.
  • Personalized Guide Interaction: Your guide can still offer a high degree of personalized attention, educating all three of you on gorilla behavior, conservation, and local ecology.
  • Minimized Environmental Impact: Even with three people, the trek maintains a low environmental impact, supporting conservation efforts by limiting human presence in the gorilla habitat.
  • Collaborative Photography: With three people, you can take turns capturing photos or videos, or assist each other in getting the best shots, enhancing the visual documentation of your adventure.
  • Quiet Observation: The small group size ensures a quieter trek, which can lead to more natural interactions with the gorillas and potentially closer encounters.
  • Shared Learning Journey: The group can engage in discussions with the guide, enriching the educational aspect by sharing insights and questions, fostering a collective learning environment.
  • Flexible Scheduling: Scheduling for three is still relatively flexible, allowing the group to choose times that might be optimal for gorilla viewing or align with everyone's availability.
  • Conservation Contribution: The cost of the trek for three people is shared, yet each person's contribution to conservation remains significant, with the added benefit of group camaraderie.

3 is not a crowd Benefits

  • Group Dynamics: Larger groups offer a vibrant social experience, enhancing camaraderie and shared excitement during the trek.
  • Shared Cost Efficiency: The cost per person can be lower when spread across more participants, making the experience more affordable for each individual.
  • Diverse Perspectives: With more people, you get a variety of perspectives and reactions, enriching the storytelling and memory-making aspect of the trek.
  • Enhanced Safety in Numbers: A larger group provides a sense of security, with more eyes watching out for each other and the guide managing group dynamics efficiently.
  • Group Learning: The guide can facilitate group discussions, making the educational component interactive with different viewpoints and questions.
  • Photography Support: More people mean more opportunities for group photos and assistance with capturing individual moments, with someone always available to take pictures.
  • Environmental Education: Larger groups can be more impactful in spreading awareness about conservation, as each member can become an ambassador for gorilla protection.
  • Flexibility in Activities: With more participants, activities can be diversified; some might focus on photography, others on learning, while some engage in quiet observation, providing a varied experience.
  • Community Building: These treks can foster a sense of community among participants, especially if they're part of an organized group or club, strengthening bonds through shared adventure.
  • Conservation Contribution: While the environmental impact is slightly higher, the collective contribution to conservation through trekking fees remains substantial, supporting broader conservation efforts.
